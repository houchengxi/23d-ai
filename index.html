Why builders use 23D

We focus on problems that actually cost you money on site.

1. Eliminate costly on-site errors

Uncoordinated plans from different consultants create clashes that only show up once you’re framing. That leads to emergency RFIs, change orders and delays.

Our clash detection finds these conflicts in the 3D model before construction, so issues are solved on paper, not on site.

2. Achieve budget certainty & reduce waste

Inaccurate material take-offs drive budget overruns and piles of unused lumber on site.

Our 3D model generates an automated bill of materials, giving you a solid baseline for ordering and reducing waste.

3. Streamline construction & reduce delays

Ambiguous 2D plans slow down trades and create arguments in the field.

Clear, unambiguous 3D views and optimized routing make it obvious what needs to happen, so crews can keep moving.

4. Access powerful tech at an unbeatable price

Traditional BIM software is expensive and requires specialist staff, which is out of reach for many smaller builders.

Our automation-driven workflow and AI-assisted tools make “digital twin” style planning accessible to regular residential builders.

5. Maximize headroom & protect structure

Poorly coordinated HVAC and sprinkler lines lead to ugly bulkheads and unnecessary holes in structural members.

By modeling these systems in 3D, we plan routing that eliminates bulkheads where possible and avoids risky structural penetrations.

Case study: main house + laneway

A real project where 3D coordination caught issues that 2D plans missed.

What we found

  • Conflicting headers above a large north sliding door and window.
  • Stair beams intersecting with stair runs between first and second floors.
  • Exterior walls without proper supporting beams under roof decks.
  • Beam height differences causing inconsistent wall heights in balcony and interior areas.
  • Roof beams conflicting with flat roof and stair enclosure geometry.

Why it matters

These are the kinds of issues that usually show up only when you’re mid-framing, with trades on site and schedules already tight.

By surfacing them early in a 3D model, the builder was able to coordinate fixes with the designer and engineer in advance, avoiding:

  • On-site redesigns
  • Last-minute RFIs and change orders
  • Delays cascading into following trades

The result: smoother framing, fewer surprises, and a cleaner final build.
